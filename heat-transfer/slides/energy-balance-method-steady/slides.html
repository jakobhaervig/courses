The energy balance method for steady heat transfer problems

Topic 2

One-dimenensional problems

Notation for one-dimension problems

  • A 1D plane wall is defined as being infinitely long (i.e. heat transfer only occurs in one direction)

  • It extends from $x=0$ to $x=L$
  • We discretise into $M$ nodes from $m=0$ to $m=M$
  • Distance between nodes is $\Delta x$
  • We form a volume element around each node which naturally also has width $\Delta x$

The energy balance equation

  • We sum up heat transfer to/from volume element:

    $$ \small \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Sum of heat}\\ \text{transfer rate}\\ \text{across surfaces}\\ \text{of volume element} \end{array} \right) + \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Rate of heat}\\ \text{generation}\\ \text{inside the}\\ \text{element} \end{array} \right) = \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Rate of change}\\ \text{of the energy}\\ \text{content of}\\ \text{the element} \end{array} \right) $$

  • For unsteady heat transfer, e.g. transient:

    • $$ \small \sum_\text{all surfaces} Q + \dot{G}_\text{element} = \frac{\Delta E_\text{element}}{\Delta t} $$

  • For steady heat transfer, e.g. no change in time:

    • $$ \small \sum_\text{all surfaces} Q + \dot{G}_\text{element} = 0 $$

Convention for heat transfer direction

  • Assumed direction of heat transfer is irrelevant as long as we are consistent

$$\small kA \frac{T_{m-1}-T_m}{\Delta x}-kA\frac{T_m-T_{m+1}}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_m A \Delta x=0$$
$$\small kA \frac{T_{m-1}-T_m}{\Delta x}+kA\frac{T_{m+1}-T_{m}}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_m A \Delta x=0$$

  • In both cases it reduces to same expression:

$$\small T_{m-1}-2T_m+T_{m+1}+\frac{\dot{g}_m\Delta x^2}{kA}=0$$

Boundary condition types

The four boundary condition types

  • We have to specify boundary conditions on each non-internal node. Four types exist:

    • 1. Constant temperature

        • $T_0 = c$

    • 2. Constant heat flux

        • $\dot{Q}_\text{left,surface}=kA\frac{\text{d}T}{\text{d}x}=c$

    • 3. Convection

        • $\dot{Q}_\text{left,surface}=hA\left(T_\infty-T_0 \right)=c$

    • 4. Radiation

        • $\dot{Q}_\text{left,surface}=\epsilon \sigma A \left(T_\text{surr}^4-T_0^4\right)=c$

Incorporating the different boundary conditions

  • For BC type 1 (constant temperature), we simply fix the temperature
  • For BC type 2-4, we set up an energy balance on the volume element: $$ \small \begin{aligned} \small \sum_\text{all surfaces} \dot{Q} + \dot{G}_\text{element} = 0, \end{aligned} $$
    • which, in the case of a 1D plane wall, expands to

      • $$\small \dot{Q}_\text{left,surface} + kA \frac{T_1-T_0}{\Delta x}+\dot{g}_0\left(\frac{\Delta x}{2}A\right)=0.$$

  • Expression for $\dot{Q}_\text{left,surface}$ depends on BC type

Incorporating constant heat flux BC

  • A constant heat flux given by $\dot{q}$:

    • $$\small \dot{Q}_\text{left,surface} = \dot{q}_0 A$$

  • Energy balance at node $m=0$ then becomes:

    • $$\small \dot{q}_0 A + k A \frac{T_1-T_0}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_0\left(\frac{\Delta x}{2}A\right) = 0$$

  • Special case of insulated boundary (zero heat flux):

    • $$\small 0 + k A \frac{T_1-T_0}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_0\left(\frac{\Delta x}{2}A\right) = 0$$

Incorporating convection BC

  • Convection with heat transfer coefficient $h$:

    • $$\small \dot{Q}_\text{left,surface} = h A \left(T_\infty-T_0\right)$$

  • Energy balance at node $m=0$ then becomes:

    • $$\small h A \left(T_\infty-T_0\right) + k A \frac{T_1-T_0}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_0\left(\frac{\Delta x}{2}A\right) = 0$$

Incorporating radiation BC

  • Radiation with emissivity $\epsilon$ and Stefan-Boltzmann constant $\sigma$:

    • $$\small \dot{Q}_\text{left,surface} = \epsilon \sigma A \left(T_\text{surr}^4-T_0^4\right)$$

  • Energy balance at node $m=0$ then becomes:

    • $$\small \epsilon \sigma A \left(T_\text{surr}^4-T_0^4\right) + k A \frac{T_1-T_0}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_0\left(\frac{\Delta x}{2}A\right) = 0$$

  • Note that: For radiation the term $\small T_0^4$ makes the equation set non-linear. Different solution method is therefore typically needed.

Incorporating mixed boundary condition types

  • All BC types (heat flux, convection and radiation) can easily be added to form mixed types:

    • $$\small \dot{Q}_\text{left,surface}=$$

    $$\small~\dot{q}_0 A$$

    $$~+$$

    $$\small~h A \left(T_\infty-T_0\right)$$

    $$~+$$

    $$\small~\epsilon \sigma A \left(T_\text{surr}^4-T_0^4\right)$$

Interfaces between different materials

  • Starting with the general energy balance:
    • $$ \small \begin{aligned} \small \sum_\text{all surfaces} \dot{Q} + \dot{G}_\text{element} = 0, \end{aligned} $$

    We may assume perfect contact (no air gaps) at the interface between materials:

    $$\small k_A A \frac{T_{m-1}-T_m}{\Delta x} + k_B A \frac{T_{m+1}-T_m}{\Delta x} + ...$$

    $$\dot{g}_{A,m}A \frac{\Delta x}{2} + \dot{g}_{B,m}A \frac{\Delta x}{2} = 0$$

Two- and Three-dimenensional problems

Notation for two-dimension problems

  • We use double subscripts ($m$,$n$) to denote two dimensions (triple for three dimensions)
    • $m$ used for $x$-direction
    • $n$ used for $y$-direction
  • Temperature at node ($m=5$, $n=4$) is written $T_{5,4}$
  • Distances between nodes are $\Delta x$ and $\Delta y$
  • We form volume elements around each node
  • We convert from node number to actual coordinates by:
    • $x=m\Delta x$
    • $y=n\Delta y$

The energy balance equation

  • We sum up heat transfer to/from volume element:

    $$ \small \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Sum of heat}\\ \text{transfer rate}\\ \text{across surfaces}\\ \text{of volume element} \end{array} \right) + \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Rate of heat}\\ \text{generation}\\ \text{inside the}\\ \text{element} \end{array} \right) = \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Rate of change}\\ \text{of the energy}\\ \text{content of}\\ \text{the element} \end{array} \right) $$

    $$ \small \sum_\text{all surfaces} Q + \dot{G}_\text{element} = \frac{\Delta E_\text{element}}{\Delta t} $$

$kA \frac{T_{m-1,n}-T_{m,n}}{\Delta x}$

$~+~$

$kA\frac{T_{m+1,n}-T_{m,n}}{\Delta x}$

$~+~$

$kA\frac{T_{m,n-1}-T_{m,n}}{\Delta x}$

$~+~$

$kA\frac{T_{m,n+1}-T_{m,n}}{\Delta x}$

$~=~$

$0$
