Lumped System Analysis

Topic 1

What is the lumped system assumption and why?

  • Most heat transfer problems are complex because temperature vary in both with time and space
    • In lumped systems, temperature only varies with time and not space
    $$T=f(x,y,z,t) \longrightarrow T=f(t)$$
  • Applying the lumped system assumption significantly simplifies the analysis

Starting point of lumped system analysis

  • Consider a body of arbitrary shape with:
    • Mass $m$
    • Volume $V$
    • Density $\rho$
    • Surface area $A_s$
    • Specific heat capacity $c_p$
    • Initial internal temperature $T_i$
  • At time $t=0$ the body is exposed to convective heat transfer from the surroundings with temperature $T_\infty$ and heat transfer coefficient $h$
  • Now, let's consider what happens during a small time interval $\text{d}t$

Derivation of the lumped system equation

  • Energy transferred to body during $\text{d}t$:

    • $$h A_s (T_\infty-T) \text{d}t = m c_p \text{d}T$$

    $$~=\rho V c_p \text{d}T$$

  • Because $T_\infty$ is constant, we may expand $\text{d}T$:

    • $$ - h A_s (T-T_\infty) = \rho V c_p \text{d}(T-T_\infty)$$

  • Now, intergrating from $t=0$ where $T=T_i$ to any time $t$ at which $T=T(t)$ we get:

    • $$\text{ln}\frac{T(t)-T_\infty}{T_i-T_\infty} = -\frac{h A_s (T-T_\infty)}{\rho V c_p}t$$

  • Taking exponential on both sides, we finally obtain the lumped system equation:

    • $$\frac{T(t)-T_\infty}{T_i-T_\infty} = \text{e}^{-\frac{h A_s}{\rho V c_p}t}$$

The time constant for lumped systems

  • Simplifying expression using the time constant ($1/b$):

    • $$\frac{T(t)-T_\infty}{T_i-T_\infty} = \text{e}^{-\frac{h A_s}{\rho V c_p}t}$$

    $$~=\text{e}^{-bt}$$
