The energy balance method

Notation for the energy balance method

  • A 1D plane wall is defined as being infinitely long (i.e. heat transfer only occurs in one direction)

  • It extends from $x=0$ to $x=L$
  • We discretise into $M$ nodes from $m=0$ to $m=M$
  • Distance between nodes is $\Delta x$
  • We form a volume element around each node which naturally also has width $\Delta x$

The energy balance equation

  • We sum up heat transfer to/from volume element:

    $$ \small \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Sum of heat}\\ \text{transfer rate}\\ \text{across surfaces}\\ \text{of volume element} \end{array} \right) + \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Rate of heat}\\ \text{generation}\\ \text{inside the}\\ \text{element} \end{array} \right) = \left( \begin{array}{ccc} \text{Rate of change}\\ \text{of the energy}\\ \text{content of}\\ \text{the element} \end{array} \right) $$

  • For unsteady heat transfer, e.g. transient:

    • $$ \small \sum_\text{all surfaces} Q + \dot{G}_\text{element} = \frac{\Delta E_\text{element}}{\Delta t} $$

  • For steady heat transfer, e.g. no change in time:

    • $$ \small \sum_\text{all surfaces} Q + \dot{G}_\text{element} = 0 $$

Convention for heat transfer direction

  • Assumed direction of heat transfer is irrelevant as long as we are consistent

$$\small kA \frac{T_{m-1}-T_m}{\Delta x}-kA\frac{T_m-T_{m+1}}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_m A \Delta x=0$$
$$\small kA \frac{T_{m-1}-T_m}{\Delta x}+kA\frac{T_{m+1}-T_{m}}{\Delta x} + \dot{g}_m A \Delta x=0$$

  • In both cases it reduces to same expression:

$$\small T_{m-1}-2T_m+T_{m+1}+\frac{\dot{g}_m\Delta x^2}{kA}=0$$
