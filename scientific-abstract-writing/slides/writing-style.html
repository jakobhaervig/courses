Course in Scientific abstract writing

Topic: Writing style

The abstract writing style

  • Concise language: Every word counts! Keep it short and to the point
  • No unnecessary details: Only include the most relevant information
  • Third first (rarely first person): Write in third person, avoid first person. Sometimes first person (I or we) is used to underline exactly what is new in this study
  • Past tense: Use past tense to describe what was done
  • Use correct keywords: Use keywords that are relevant to the field to help search engines find your abstract
  • Citations: Avoid citations in the abstract
